Design for America: Leading Design Teams with Social Impact Initiatives (2013-2015)
dfa.stanford.edu
Design for America is a national organization composed of student teams using the design process to create products and services with meaningful local impact. I have led teams addressing medication adherence and worker safety. It's all about design process: - Through local stakeholder interviews, we narrow our project scope from a large issue like "worker safety" to a local painpoint like "effective onboarding of new and rotating kitchen workers." - We formulate a series of "How Might We" statements and use these to brainstorm potential solutions. - We prototype these solutions and test them with local community partners. Kindred Britain: Visualizing Famous British Genealogies (2013)
kindred.stanford.edu
Kindred Britain is a data visualization project seeking to highlight surprising genealogical connections from British history. The data has many different facets that do not lend themselves well to verbal description, and target users are in an older, academic audience unlikely to be familiar with the technology supporting the site.
